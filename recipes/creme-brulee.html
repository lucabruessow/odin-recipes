Crème Brûlée

Description

Crème Brûlée is a classic French dessert that combines a creamy, vanilla-flavored custard with a crisp caramelized sugar topping. It's a perfect balance of silky smooth and crunchy textures, making it a favorite for dinner parties or special occasions.

This recipe is simple to make, requiring only a few basic ingredients. With a little patience and a kitchen torch, you can create an elegant and impressive dessert that will wow your guests.

Ingredients for 4 servings

  • 500 ml (2 cups) heavy cream
  • 5 egg yolks
  • 100 g (1/2 cup) granulated sugar, plus extra for caramelizing
  • 1 vanilla bean (or 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract)
  • Pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 160°C (325°F).

  2. Prepare the custard:

    In a saucepan, heat the cream with the vanilla bean (split and seeds scraped) or vanilla extract until it just begins to simmer. Remove from heat and let it sit for a few minutes.

  3. Whisk the egg yolks and sugar in a bowl until pale and slightly thickened. Slowly pour the warm cream into the egg mixture while whisking continuously.

  4. Strain the mixture through a fine sieve into a jug. Pour it into four ramekins.

  5. Place the ramekins in a deep baking dish. Fill the dish with hot water halfway up the sides of the ramekins (water bath).

  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the custard is just set but still slightly wobbly in the center.
  7. Remove from the water bath and let cool to room temperature. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours (or overnight).
  8. Before serving, sprinkle a thin, even layer of sugar over each custard. Use a kitchen torch to caramelize the sugar until golden and crisp. Let it sit for a minute to harden before serving.
