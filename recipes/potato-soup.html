Potato soup

Description

This vegetarian potato soup is a hearty and comforting dish, perfect for cold days or whenever you crave a warm and filling meal. Made with simple ingredients like potatoes, carrots, tofu, and onions, it’s easy to prepare and full of flavor.

The creamy broth, flavored with herbs and spices, creates a rich base that pairs perfectly with the tender vegetables. Whether you’re a vegetarian or just looking for a healthy, plant-based meal, this soup is a delicious and satisfying option that everyone will enjoy.

Ingredients for 4 servings

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 500g (1 lb) potatoes, diced
  • 3 carrots, diced
  • 200g (7 oz) firm tofu, cubed
  • 1 liter (4 cups) vegetable broth
  • 200 ml (3/4 cup) plant-based cream (e.g., oat or soy)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Prepare the vegetables:

    Dice the potatoes, carrots, and onion. Cube the tofu.

  2. Cook the base:

    Heat the olive oil in a large pot. Sauté the onion and garlic over medium heat until fragrant and translucent.

  3. Add the vegetables:

    Add the potatoes, carrots, and tofu to the pot. Stir well and cook for 5 minutes.

  4. Simmer the soup:

    Pour in the vegetable broth. Add thyme, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let the soup simmer for 20–25 minutes until the vegetables are tender.

  5. Finish the soup:

    Stir in the plant-based cream and adjust seasoning to taste. Let it warm through for a few minutes without boiling.

  6. Garnish with chopped parsley and serve hot.
